Hidden Data Files

Timestamps, GPS locations, friend lists, and chat logs (stored as JSON files)

visibility_off What Snapchat Does NOT Include

  • Snapchat strips all date and location data from your photos. Every exported file shows the download date — not when it was taken. The real dates and GPS coordinates are hidden in separate data files. This is exactly why Snatched exists.
  • Overlays are separate. Filters, stickers, text, and drawings are exported as separate PNG files — NOT baked into your photos. Snatched automatically merges them back together.
  • Expired and deleted Snaps are gone. Only content saved to Memories is exportable.
  • Chat media is limited. The chat export includes text logs and some media, but not every image/video ever sent.
Getting Started

Getting to "My Data"

phone_iphone From the Snapchat App

  1. Open Snapchat and tap your profile icon / Bitmoji (top-left)
  2. Tap the gear icon (top-right) to open Settings
  3. Scroll to Privacy Controls
  4. Tap "My Data"
  5. This opens accounts.snapchat.com in your browser
  6. Log in again if prompted

computer From a Computer

  1. Go to accounts.snapchat.com
  2. Log in with your Snapchat credentials (+ 2FA if enabled)
  3. Click "My Data" from the menu

We recommend using a computer for the download — ZIP files can be several gigabytes.

schedule First-time notice: If this is the first time accessing My Data from a new device, Snapchat may impose a 72-hour security hold before processing your request. Plan ahead.

Troubleshooting

Common Issues

"I never got the email"

Check your spam/junk folder. You don't need the email — go directly to accounts.snapchat.com > My Data > See exports to download.

"The download link expired"

Request a new export (same steps). Download immediately next time — links expire after 72 hours. This is why we recommend requesting ALL data in one export.

"My export is missing Memories / no photos or videos"

Make sure "Export your Memories" was toggled ON at the top of the page. If you only see JSON files and chat media, you exported without the Memories toggle.

"Snatched says no metadata found"

Make sure "Export JSON Files" was toggled ON. Without this, you only get raw media — Snatched needs the JSON files to restore dates and locations.

"I hit the daily request limit"

Snapchat limits export requests per day. Wait 24 hours and try again. Select ALL categories and ALL date ranges in one request to avoid this.

"Snapchat says try again in 72 hours"

This is a security measure for new/unrecognized devices. Wait the 72 hours and try from the same device.

"The ZIP is huge and my upload is slow"

Large exports (5+ GB) take time. Use a wired connection if possible. Snatched shows upload progress. Consider uploading from a computer rather than a phone.

"My photos all show today's date in my gallery"

This is exactly the problem Snatched solves. Snapchat strips original dates — they only exist in JSON metadata. Upload to Snatched and it embeds the correct dates back via EXIF.

"My exported photos are smaller/lower quality than my camera roll originals"

This is a Snapchat limitation, not Snatched. Snapchat's data export delivers photos at lower quality than the originals saved to your camera roll (DCIM/Snapchat). Files from Snapchat's servers are pre-compressed at JPEG quality 61–80 — significantly lower than the Q90+ originals your phone saved at capture time.

Worse, data has been shown to shrink over time as Snapchat re-encodes stored media on their servers. The longer you wait to export, the more quality you may lose.

Snatched preserves every byte Snapchat gives us. Photos without overlays are copied byte-for-byte (only EXIF metadata is added). Photos with overlays are re-composited at quality 95 to minimize loss. We never downgrade your files.
